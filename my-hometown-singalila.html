    My Hometown in Singalila Forest

    • By Zadie Ackerman
    • November 28th, 2021
    • TravelLiterature

    Singalila National Park is an Indian national park. It is in the Darjeeling district, West Bengal, near the Indo-Nepal border. It is one of India's most fascinating Himalayan forests, which has fascinating mammals like red pandas, barking deer, clouded leopard, black panther, leopard and Himalayan serow. It also has over 300 species of birds, making it a birder's paradise. Some exotic bird species found here include blood pheasant, kalij pheasant, satyr tragopan and fulvous parrotbills. The park becomes colourful with over 20 types of rhododendrons from March to April and over 600 orchid varieties (the world's most extensive concentration in a single geographical area) from October to November.
    Along the Singalila Ridge, Singalila National Park is at a high altitude between 2,100 to 3,650 metres above sea level. It is well known for the trekking route to Sandakphu, one of the highest peaks in West Bengal. It offers one of the best places for viewing astonishing sunrises and sunsets along with Kanchenjunga.
    Rimbik, my hometown, is a beautiful hamlet at 7500 ft above sea level situated at the northwest end of Darjeeling district. It is the last market of Singalila National Park. It is famous for its natural vegetation and rich resources and is the oldest trekking halt for trekkers. The inhabitants of this village are honest, kind and diligent. They mainly speak Nepali. If you are a trekker, this is the ideal place to go on a trek without noise and pollution. Sandakphu, Moley, Phalut, Gorkhey are nearby destinations from Rimbik.
