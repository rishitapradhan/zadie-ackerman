    A Book For Every Grade

    • By Zadie Ackerman
    • November 24th, 2021
    • BooksLiterature

    “If one cannot enjoy reading a book over and over again, there is no use in reading it at all.” Here is a list of books you will love reading. These book scan be read in schools, libraries, home learning, EVERYWHERE. This list has been compiled to include fiction and non-fiction books for all abilities, including reluctant readers and the ablest - with an emphasis on fun and thought-provoking reads.

    Grade 1: Meet Yasmin! - Saadia Faruqi

    Yasmin is a lively 2nd grader who's always on the lookout for hints to help her overcome life's small obstacles. She takes inspiration from around her, and using her imagination, boldly faces any situation. She is an imaginative thinker and a curious explorer. Yasmin and her multi-generational family will delight and inspire the readers.

    Grade 2: Owl Moon -Jane Yolen

    This story is about a little girl and her father, who go owling on a moonlit winter night. As the father and daughter walk through the woods searching for the great horned owl, the daughter discovers many things about how a winter night feels and how much can be learned from listening and watching. This simple yet telling story explores the magic of winter and the love between a parent and child.

    Grade 3: Crenshaw -Katherine Applegate

    Crenshaw is a novel about Jackson, a young boy whose family is struggling with financial instability. His imaginary friend, Crenshaw, is a human-sized cat with a fondness for bubble baths and doing cartwheels. The story explores the impact of poverty on family and community, the need to reconcile fact and fancy, and how to face the unknown productively.

    Grade 4:Wonder -R. J. Palacio

    Wonder is a memorable story of kindness, courage and wonder. After being home-schooled for years, Auggie Pullman is about to start fifth grade. But, he is worried about How he will fit in. Auggie has had 27 surgeries to correct the facial anomalies he was born with, but still has a face that has earned him cruel nicknames. In spite of all that, Auggie is a smart, funny, kind and brave kid. This novel is divided into different points of view, demonstrating that his arrival at school doesn’t test only him, it affects everyone in the community.

    Grade 5: Coraline -Neil Gaiman

    When Coraline moves to an old house, she feels bored and neglected by her parents. She finds a hidden door with a bricked up passage. During the night, she crosses the passage and finds a parallel world where everybody has buttons instead of eyes, with caring parents and all her dreams coming true. When the Other Mother invites Coraline to stay in her world forever, the girl refuses and finds that the alternate reality where she is trapped is only a trick to lure her.

    Grade 6: Beetle Boy -MG Leonard

    Thirteen-year-old Darkus Cuttle’s search for his kidnapped father leads him to discover a mountain of extraordinary beetles, and pits him against the mad-scientist of the fashion world, Lucretia Cutter, an haute couture villainess with an alarming interest in insects. This is a darkly hilarious adventure full of exotic beetles, daring schemes, and true friendship.

    Grade 7: The Girl Who Drank the Moon -Kelly Barnhill

    Every year, the people of the Protectorate leave a baby as an offering to keep the witch from terrorizing their town. But the witch, Xan, is kind and gentle as rescues the abandoned children and delivers them to welcoming families on the other side of the forest, nourishing the babies with starlight on the journey. One year, Xan accidentally feeds a baby moonlight 'enmagickening' Luna. Xan decides she must raise her as her own. As Luna's 13th birthday approaches, her magic begins to emerge-with dangerous consequences

    Grade 8: The Boy in the Striped Pyjamas -John Boyne

    Bruno (Asa Butterfield) and his family flee Berlin during World War II to settle near the concentration camp where his father (David Thewlis) has recently been appointed commandant. He sneaks out behind his house one day, unhappy and lonely, and meets Shmuel (Jack Scanlon), a Jewish youngster his age. Despite the camp's barbed-wire fence separating them, the boys form an illicit connection, oblivion to the true nature of their surroundings.

    Grade 9: Life of Pi - Yann Martel

    Santosh and Gita Patel board a freighter with their sons and a few remaining animals after deciding to sell their zoo in India and move to Canada. When the ship is wrecked by a violent storm, the Patels' teenage son, Pi (Suraj Sharma), is the only human survivor. Pi isn't alone; a ferocious Bengal tiger has found refuge in the lifeboat too. Pi and the tiger must learn to trust each other as days turn into weeks and weeks turn into months if they are to survive.

    Grade 10: The Book Thief -Markus Zusak

    The Book Thief is a story narrated by a 'compassionate Death' who tells us about Liesel, a girl growing up in Germany during World War II. She steals books, learns to read, and finds comfort in words. She and Max, the Jew her family protects, are the only main characters that survive the war.

    Grade 11: Julie of the Wolves -Jean Craighead George

    Miyax, a 13-year-old Eskimo girl, must choose between sticking to her tribe and the boy she has been forced to marry or fleeing to San Francisco, where she might be able to live with a pen pal she has never met. She decides to flee, but she becomes hopelessly lost in the Alaskan tundra. Her only chance of surviving is to be accepted into a wolf pack. This is a terrific adventure tale! Julie and Julie's Wolf Pack are the sequels to this book.

    Grade 12: The Kite Runner -Khaled Hosseini

    The Kite Runner, written by Khaled Hosseini, tells the extraordinary story of an unlikely friendship between a wealthy Afghan boy, named Amir, and Hassan, the son of his father's servant — who he later finds out, after Hassan's death, has always been his half-brother.
